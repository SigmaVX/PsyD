Specializations

I have a dual specialization in School and Clinical Psychology. School psychology is concerned with the practice of psychology of children, youth, families, learners of all ages and the schooling process. Clinical psychology is a branch of psychology concerned with the treatment and assessment of mental illness.


Learn More About Our Philosophy

Children

I treat children ages 3-18 dealing with depression, anxiety, trauma, ADHD, divorce, school, and peer challenges. Treatment involves the use of play, social skills and problem-solving, cognitive behavioral interventions and collateral parent sessions.


Young Adults

I work with young adults ages 18-25 dealing with depression, anxiety, trauma, ADHD, identity and relationship issues. Treatment involves self-discovery, understanding attachment styles, and relationships.


Family

Whole family sessions address family goals, communication, boundary setting, conflict resolution and strengthen attachment and attunement. The main goal of all family work is to heal what is hurting and to strengthen and build the resiliency of the family system.


Women's Issues

I have a specific interest in women's life experiences that enhance or hinder her mental health. Treatment may focus on identity, role shifts, self-esteem, empowerment, discovering and holding on to voice, trauma, relationships etc.

Overview

Dr. Michele Cannata is a licensed clinical psychologist with a Psy.D. degree in School/Clinical Child Psychology from Pace University. She has provided mental health services in various settings including inpatient and outpatient hospital settings, residential treatment facilities, the public school system, and private practice.

Background

Extensive training in child therapy throughout her internship, post doctoral supervision (in a private practice) and school based experiences with knowledge in play therapy, trauma, learning challenges, parenting and family work.

Focus
