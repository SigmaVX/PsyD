Philosophy

I am often asked how does "it" work. I suspect people are asking how do I get better? How does recovery or healing happen? The process of psychotherapy is not easily described. The relationship between the patient and the therapist is the primary healing factor. The unconditional regard, safe and nonjudgmental relationship is the platform to explore early attachment styles and current patterns in the patient's relationships outside of the therapy office. Connection with others is a core part of being human and I am side by side with my patients in improving this aspect of their lives.

I do strongly believe in positive psychology, which is the study of the strengths that enable individuals to thrive. I encourage my patients to cultivate what is best within themselves and to embrace love and kindness for themselves and others.

Children

Children are living their parent-child attachments while simultaneously developing connections with others outside of the home. These relationships are explored through play, storytelling, art, and conversation. Common themes are power and control, individuality, boundaries, trust, defining friendships, and am I good enough.

Young Adults

This is a unique time in life. They are in between the dependency of childhood and independence of adulthood. They are starting to feel responsible for themselves but still closely tied to their parents and family. Common themes are identity, romantic relationships, long term friendships, self-care and stress management.

Family

We are all embedded in a family context. Strengthening the attachments we have is the main goal of family work. This occurs with improved communication, conflict resolutions and discovering how to express and receive love.
