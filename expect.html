What To Expect

Psychotherapy is not easy to describe. It is a highly personal experience. Here are a few things you can expect from my practice.

FAQ

Q: What is psychotherapy? Psychotherapy is not easily described in words. It is a highly personal experience that leads to self awareness and positive behavioral changes. Q: What type of therapist are you? I am an eclectic therapist who uses a variety of techniques to improve a patient's well being. I do emphasize psychodynamic and interpersonal theory. I strive to offer a clean, calm, warm and inviting office setting. Confidentiality and privacy are valued and deemed essential. I hope to engage my patients in a safe, trusting, non-judgmental partnership to support self-discovery. Therapy is hard emotional work and I offer support and compassion through the process. I am an active therapist who will remain involved in your treatment and healing. Q: What changes can I expect? Therapeutic gains include improved overall well being. You may feel that you have improved communication of thoughts and feelings, more satisfying interpersonal relationships and self esteem. Your sleep and energy may improve as well. Q: How long is an appointment? An initial appointment, referred to as an intake, is an hour in length and involves question and answer. Individual sessions there after are 45 minutes in length. Q: How much does psychotherapy cost? The initial appointment (an hour) is $190.00. 45-minute sessions are $170.00. Q: Will the sessions be covered by my health insurance? I am an out of network provider. This means that I do not participate in insurance panels. If your policy allows for out of network coverage you may be reimbursed for the sessions. It is helpful to call your insurance company and ask if your policy has out of network coverage for behavioral health and what your deductible is. If you do have out of network coverage and the insurance company accepts electronic claims (most do) I am happy to submit the claims for you. I do work with my patients to make therapy something they can commit to both in time and money. Q: How frequent are the sessions and how long will I be in therapy? The sessions are typically weekly. This allows for the development of trust and a working relationship at a faster rate so that therapeutic gains can be realized sooner. Every other week sessions are a possibility. I typically suggest stepping down to every other week as improvement is seen and we are entering the termination phase of treatment. The length of psychotherapy is very individual. Some people come in for a specific, targeted problem that may be alleviated with a short term model. It is more common that the therapeutic process is long term with constant monitoring of personal goals and the growth process. The ending of therapy is frequently discussed in treatment.
